Somewhere between a bench synopsis and a counter-affidavit, I started keeping notes that did not belong in either.

Not case notes. Not research memos. Something closer to thinking out loud about why a judge reasoned the way they did, whether a drafting choice in an agreement would survive a dispute, or what it means to study law in a system still sorting out its own identity.

This blog is that margin, made public.

I am a law student with a concentration in corporate law and contract drafting. Over the last two years I have worked across arbitration chambers, trademark practice, High Court internships, and transactional advisory. That is enough to know that the most interesting legal questions rarely have clean answers.

The posts here will reflect that. Some will be close readings of legal problems I have worked through. Some will be observations from practice. A few might be wrong. All of them will be honest.

If you are reading this while considering me for an internship or a role, the writing is the point. A CV lists what I have done. This is how I think.
